IMA3 : Video Production - Using colors as cognitive tools for language teaching and learning

   

Performance Problem analysis: The idea of developing an instructional video on using colors as cognitive tools for language teaching and learning came to me when I was teaching French to my students in India. Using simple and natural elements such as colors helps language teachers make students learn second language with ease. The students are also more in control of their learning process as they are using colors that they are familiar with to bring meaning to ideas they are unfamiliar with.

   

Description of the Instructional Video: In the video, I highlighted how colors can be used as cognitive tools for teaching and learning a foreign language. I analysed how colors can be used as components of structure, building blocks in grammatical structure, chronological elements, and metacognitive tools. I also included an evaluation at the end of the video to help viewers reinforce and assess their understanding. I used Movavi Video Editor which is a Windows Movie Editor to use relevant images and integrate them with my narration.

   

Educational value of the Instructional video: Colors are powerful visual stimuli that can help learners associate abstract concepts with concrete, familiar elements. By integrating colors into language learning, the video helps students make connections between new language concepts and their existing knowledge, facilitating better retention and understanding. The video helps language teachers use constructivist learning and is useful to learners with different preferences and diverse backgrounds.
